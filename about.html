Our Story

A family, a 250-year-old house, and a city of mystics.

Home · Our Story

The Way Inn is the home of Chef Rony BarEl and his wife Janine — a 250-year-old stone house in the Artists' Quarter of Safed that they slowly turned, room by room, into a boutique hotel.

Rony cooks; Janine hosts. Mornings are warm bread and a Galilean table; for groups, the chef opens the stone hall for Mediterranean dinners. Guests tend to remember the food, the views and the feeling of being looked after.

They named the ten suites for the Sephirot of the Tree of Life — because in Safed, the birthplace of Kabbalah, the map of the house and the map of the soul are the same map.
